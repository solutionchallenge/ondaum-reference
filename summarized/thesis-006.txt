Summary:

The study by Brown et al. (2023) explores the lived experiences of mental health service users, focusing on how they navigate cognitive dissonance arising from conflicting beliefs about their mental health, treatment, and self-identity. Using a qualitative design, the researchers conducted in-depth interviews with 15 participants and analyzed the data thematically. Key findings revealed that participants frequently encountered dissonance due to societal stigma, internal contradictions about their illness, and ambivalence toward medical interventions. To cope, individuals engaged in meaning-making processes, reframed personal narratives, and sought validation through peer support.
